V33A-03 Rapid experimental determination of magmatic phase equilibria: coordinating a volcanic crisis response protocol

    Wednesday, 11 December 2019 Wednesday, 11 December 2019

    14:10 - 14:25 14:10 - 14:25

    • 153, Upper Mezz. (Moscone South)
Swirl Topics
  • Extreme Events & Hazards - SWIRL
Abstract
Interpreting magmatic storage conditions recorded by the texture, mineralogy, and composition of volcanic rocks often requires a petrologic phase diagram. Comparison with natural samples provides a means of describing temporal variability in those conditions, and, in combination with other analyses, such data can inform estimates of syn-eruption decompression rates. Should an eruption begin, or a volcano show signs of unrest, rapid determination of phase equilibria at crustal P-T conditions will benefit monitoring agencies and the broader research community. To demonstrate the feasibility of using multiple laboratories to produce a phase diagram in a 2-week timeframe, and develop protocols for applying this approach to future episodes of volcanic unrest, 5 experimental petrology laboratories (Alaska, Baylor, Hawaii, Smithsonian, Texas) will conduct laboratory phase equilibrium experiments in concert. The simulation will begin with overnight shipment of Mount Augustine low-K dacite (~7645 cal. yr. BP) samples (to include both a pumice chip and an aliquot of crushed pumice for use in experiments) from the USGS Alaska Volcano Observatory to each lab. Each lab will run 4 H2O-saturated experiments in Waspalloy vessels (fO2 =~NNO+1) at pre-assigned P-T conditions (from 50-200 MPa and 800-925 °C) for durations of ~96 hours. At least one experiment from each lab will duplicate that of another, allowing inter-lab comparisons. Experimental run products will then be shipped overnight to the Smithsonian Institution for petrological analysis by EPMA and SEM; the natural pumice will be analyzed during the same session. Reversal experiments will be performed at Smithsonian to better determine phase stabilities and bracket equilibrium compositions. USGS Menlo Park will analyze melt inclusions to provide an independent estimate of pre-eruption volatile contents and fluid pressures. We anticipate completing the phase diagram and analyses within 2 weeks of receiving samples. Our study will evaluate the feasibility and identify the challenges of a rapid experimental petrology response to volcanic unrest.
